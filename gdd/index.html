1Vision

Point Defense is a Last Stand power fantasy. Your weapons tear through the horde — and the horde keeps coming. The odds are grim, new bullshit keeps happening, and the delight lives exactly in that collision: !FUN! meets power fantasy, the immovable object versus the ever-increasing unstoppable force. You are the Point — one tower, the whole screen against you — and every system in the game exists to make the stand feel mighty while the pressure never stops mounting.

Law · Focus
There should always be more worth doing than you can do, and at least two defensible answers to "what now?" A no-brainer choice is a design bug.

The core resource is focus / prioritization: where your weapons point, which upgrade you take, which weapons you lean on. Triage and tradeoffs — at every timescale, from a half-second aim decision to a season-long grind target. Most of this document is that one idea applied at different zoom levels.

Audience

Household artifact, for now. The game is tuned for two named players' joy, not retention curves; "readable" means their thumbs specifically; scope decisions ask "does the household want it," not "does a market." The "for now" is an explicit revisit trigger — shipping to strangers would reopen onboarding, difficulty, and accessibility assumptions.

2The Run

The anatomy of a stand:

  • Escalation. Waves compound in density, speed, and modifier cruelty (§5). The pressure curve only rises.
  • The end boss. Around wave 50–100 — placed by content density, not dogma: the boss arrives when the game has finished showing you its bullshit. TENTATIVE on the exact wave.
  • The stand holds. A run can be won. The object was truly immovable. This is the fantasy's payoff, not its betrayal.
  • The endless encore. FUTURE Post-victory, endless mode — where death resumes its old job and depth is the only scoreboard.
  • Death pays. Every run ends in shards scaled by distance. The loss screen is a shop door, not a punishment. Losing must always buy something.
Law · Death-shape
Death is drowning, never invoicing. A valid death is your clear-rate visibly losing the race to the horde. An invalid death is bookkeeping — chipped down over minutes while winning every visible fight. Death must trace to a felt moment of being outpaced.

Chips are signals. A single leaker taking a bite out of the tower is information — "your line has a hole" — not meaningful attrition. Recoverable HP (level-up heals, §7; regen effects) is what keeps chip damage in the signal business and out of the death business.

3The Focus Economy

Law · Delegation
Power is delegation; skill is prioritization. Progress automates the bottom of your kill chain — never your attention.

Early game, your thumb is the weapon: aim matters, every tap is a decision. As a run (and an account) matures, auto weapons absorb chaff-clearing — but autos are deliberately insufficient on their own. What they buy you is not rest; it is the freedom to spend every second of attention on the decisions that matter. The veteran is a conductor, not a gardener: hands busy every few seconds, all of it high-leverage.

Aim-time is the endgame currency. Every manual weapon is priced in seconds-of-focus; threats are designed as competing bids for those seconds. Elites, armor, and regen exist to be expensive in focus; chaff exists to be cheap in autos.

CANONICAL TARGET EXPERIENCE — the veteran's ninety seconds (default tower) My beam cuts through the enemy horde, destroying all but the toughest ones in mere seconds, but the swarm is approaching from every direction at once. The frost aura and orbitals slow their progress immensely when they approach the Point, but it won't last forever, and it is not enough for my auto-weapons to take all of them out. I sweep my beam to a cluster of enemies near the orbitals and clear them out, then the next, then the next — but that allows some high-priority enemies to inch closer; elites with armor or regen will easily make their way to the tower if I don't focus them from further out. A single fast slips through and takes a chip off my defense — no big deal, the regen will restore it before too long. The trouble is that it could happen at all. Sir Cumference appears at the edge of the screen — I could focus him down, but I cannot afford to leave my aim on him for that long. I swipe a force wall down in front of a cluster, leaving them stuck in the orbitals' path, hoping it'll be enough; swipe another wall in front of Sir Cumference; then focus my beam on taking down the regenning elites. Sir Cumference shakes himself and more enemies fly out from his sides, heading for the Point. Dammit — I need to eliminate them as well, but if I don't focus the boss he'll walk straight through; the slow and knockbacks barely register on him. I've taken several additional chips down to less than half health now, but most of the regular enemies are done — now it's a matter of balancing focus between the boss and his spawns.

Note what the narration establishes: the hands never retire; chips are absorbed and noted, not feared; the boss forces a focus dilemma that CC cannot dissolve; and every sentence contains a tradeoff. Tower choice may bend this archetype (§7) — this is the default tower's answer.

4Combat Grammar

Gestures are weapon classes

Tap/aim ("gun"), hold (channel), swipe (field) — plus autos. The gesture vocabulary teaches itself through play; no gesture is ever a dead input (everything also updates the aim point).

ClassRoleJudged by
AutosInfrastructure — absorb the bottom of the kill chain, shape the flow (slows, grinders, zones)Throughput; being reliably insufficient alone
Gun / hold (manual)The scalpel — the top of the kill chain: elites, priority targets, bossesDamage per second-of-focus
Swipe / fieldTempo — board control, time-buying, combo setups (a wall pinning a cluster in the orbitals' grind path) Seconds purchased and setups created — not DPS

Slot grammar

A build fields at most 1 gun · 1 hold · 1 swipe · up to 3 autos (numbers tunable, and tower-bendable — see §7). The caps are the focus law applied to builds: you cannot own everything, so identity is forced by scarcity. Power comes from depth, not collection. TENTATIVE on exact numbers.

Forms are texture

Mastery-unlocked weapon forms (§7) change a weapon's rhythm and behavior, not just its numbers — burst-fire's ratatatata-pause-ratatatata versus the default's bang-pause-bang. The mastery reward should be audible and felt, not merely arithmetic.

5Threat Design

Law · Legibility
Shape = species · highlight = modifier · fill = allegiance. Hectic is the plan; unclear is the enemy. A mechanic that cannot be read at a glance mid-fight is wrong even if it is fun on paper.

Species are roles in the focus economy

Each base shape exists to make a distinct bid for your attention: chaff that autos eat (grunts), speed that punishes inattention (darts), mass that clogs (tanks), death that multiplies (splitters), and focus-sinks that must be answered manually (elites). Stats live in the spec tier; the role is the design truth.

The modifier grammar

Modifiers (variants) are threats, not trades — the highlight means "worse," full stop. Each occupies a distinct visual channel, which is what makes stacking legible:

ChannelModifierMeaning
Glow outlineSwiftSpeed
Thick outlineArmoredBulk
Inner glyph (green plus)RegenHeals — a focus-sink: partial damage is wasted damage
Rotating ringShieldedFirst hits ignored — punishes chip-spam, rewards sustained focus
Pulsing core (the only fill)VolatileMedic-bomb on death — target-priority becomes a real decision

The wave-40 regime change: modifiers compound

Past roughly wave 40, escalation changes gear: returning bosses carry guaranteed modifiers announced as epithets ("SIR CUMFERENCE, THE ARMORED"), and regular spawns begin rolling stacked multi-modifier enemies — armor + fast + regen is going to be hell. Legibility survives because the channels are disjoint: three modifiers read as three channels lit on one silhouette.

Law · Bosses
Bosses can be slowed, never displaced. You may buy time against a boss; you may not deny it. The arrival is inevitable — only the schedule negotiates.

Bosses are focus-forcers: threats that cannot be delegated to autos and will not wait. Slow works on them (the counter-boss tool stays honest); knockback barely registers (no exponential CC-lock). Signature behaviors beyond the ram — adds, bursts, surges — are load-bearing for the endgame, not flavor: each boss should pose a different focus dilemma.

Content doctrine: composition is the engine

Escalating composition plus a growing modifier pool is the default bullshit generator. The game gets crueler by mixing known ingredients denser and meaner — new species, new modifiers, deeper stacks. Run-random events and curveballs are a named FUTURE organ, wanted eventually, not designed now.

6The Meta Loop

Death → shards → lattice → next stand. Weapon use → mastery exp → weapon trees. Tower use → tower trees. Three currencies, one destination:

The organizing principle: the account authors the draft. Meta-progression's true payout is not bigger numbers — it is richer future decks. Lattice unlocks inject card types into the in-run pool (crit cards exist only after the crit node); mastery trees inject form cards; loadout priority fixes slot identity (§7). Everything you grind changes what your future runs can offer you.

Law · No-meta-accel
Nothing purchasable may speed up meta-progression — no +shards, no mastery-exp boosters. Managing the tradeoff between in-run power and meta rewards is not fun. (In-run xp multipliers stay legal: leveling speed is in-game power, not meta speed.)
Law · QoL-milestone
Quality-of-life is milestone-granted, never bought. Convenience unlocks arrive automatically at total-investment thresholds (own your third hold weapon → hold priority unlocks). QoL never competes with power in a shop — so it is never a feel-bad and never a no-brainer.

The account arc is finite. A deep grind — lattice, towers, masteries — then an afterlife of challenge achievements and build-chasing. The replayability bar is OPEN, deliberately: we do not need infinite replayability now, so no system is warped to fake it.

Respecs are free (at least for now): identity lives in current choices, not sunk costs.

7Builds

A build is lattice passives + tower abilities + selected weapons + their upgrades, decided across three layers: the workshop authors who you are; the battle still deals the cards.

Layer 1 — Progression (between runs)

Shards into the lattice (passive power and card-pool unlocks); use-earned exp into weapon trees (forms, behavior changes) and tower trees (special abilities).

Layer 2 — Loadout (pre-run)

  • Tower = chassis. Towers bend the slot grammar itself — the "gunslinger" runs 2 guns + 2 autos — and carry tree-unlocked abilities. Tower identity is "how does this tower change the ninety seconds."
  • Starting form, when unlocked: begin the run with your bolt already in buckshot form.
  • Weapon priority, when unlocked TENTATIVE: deterministic slot resolution — you don't control when a hold-weapon offer appears, but when one does, it is your flamethrower. The run can say "not yet"; it can no longer say "no." Randomness survives in tempo, dies in identity.
  • Mature-tree start: enough total investment lets a veteran start at ~level 20 / wave 20, clicking through banked picks — the solved early game becomes a pre-run ritual instead of a chore.

Layer 3 — The draft (in battle)

Level-up: time stops (the pause is mercy-punctuation, not a focus violation). Three cards, plus a ~10% heal TENTATIVE on the number — which quietly builds a doom clock into late runs: the xp curve stretches, the heals rarify, exactly when pressure peaks. A 4th card slot and a reroll are named FUTURE maybes.

Card types: new-weapon-for-empty-slot · weapon level · generic passive (pool authored by the lattice) · form cards — offered only when a weapon is max-level and the form is mastery-unlocked; rare but recurring within a run.

CANONICAL TARGET EXPERIENCE — the wave-30 draft Time stops. Three cards. One is +10% critical hit chance — not a default passive; it joined the pool when we unlocked crits on the lattice. One is the level-5 upgrade for the flamethrower. And, since our gun is maxed at level 6, we were lucky enough to draw a variant card that lets us change it from the default form into the burst — which we unlocked on the gun tree. We take the small heal from the level-up, choose the burst, and it's back into the fray, where our burst-fire now plows through the shapes with a satisfying ratatatata-pause-ratatatata instead of the previous bang-pause-bang.

The draft's tension taxonomy

Every hard pick weighs three currencies against each other:

  • Power now — the crit card, the DPS patch, the heal you need.
  • Build completion — flamethrower 6; the engine coming online.
  • The form gamble — decline burst, hold out for buckshot: paying present power for preferred identity, a bet placed against the dice mid-drowning. Pure !FUN!.

Draft memory/pity (guaranteed offers for empty slots, form-card pity timers) is OPEN — spec-tier once the priority mechanic firms up.

8Visual & Audio Language

  • Tron/cyber: simple, shiny, geometric. Neon strokes on dark; clean silhouettes; the legibility law (§5) is the aesthetic, not a constraint on it.
  • Radials are the motif. Everything radiates from the Point — the arena, the threats, the attention model, and (pinned: probably again) the lattice. The tech screen should read as the Point's own defense grid.
  • Juice with restraint: shake, flash, bursts — loud enough that a siege or a chip is felt, never so loud that legibility drowns.
  • Rhythm is loot. Weapon forms pay out in sound and cadence (§4); the ear should know your build.
  • Haptics on tower hits and boss arrivals: FUTURE (pinned).

9Onboarding

  • First death arrives within player levels ~5–10 — the first tech-tree visit is minutes away, not a quarter-hour. The shop door opens early.
  • Content is drip-fed: roughly one new shape or modifier every 2–3 waves, stretching past wave 20. Every first sighting of the run fires an introduction banner + highlight ring — a tutorial beat, not a trophy (it repeats by design). Forever-firsts feed the bestiary.
  • Veterans skip what they've solved via the mature-tree start (§7) — onboarding pacing never has to compromise with veteran impatience, because veterans exit the onboarding waves entirely.

10Futures & Open Questions

Named organs, explicitly not commitments:

  • FUTURE Co-op: one Point, two loadouts — separate picks, separately-colored fire; ownership without forking the geometry (pinned with a sequenced plan).
  • FUTURE Run-random events — curveballs and mutators, once composition alone stops carrying the !FUN!.
  • FUTURE Endless mode design — what the encore scores and how it escalates.
  • FUTURE 4th draft card · reroll · more slot grammar benders.
Open, deliberately:
  • The replayability bar — what the veteran chases when content runs out. Unset on purpose; no system may be warped to fake infinity.
  • End-boss placement (50–100) — decided by content density when we get there.
  • Priority mechanics' final shape; draft memory/pity.
  • Tower archetype roster beyond the gunslinger sketch.
  • Exact slot-grammar numbers; heal-per-level number.
